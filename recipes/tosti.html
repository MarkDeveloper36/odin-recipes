Description

Famous Dutch food writer Johannes van Dam called it 'The national caf√© and barfood for the last centuries'. And he was so right. The tosti is a warm comfort on a rainy day, a cheap lunch for Dutch students and a quick fix when you stayed at a bar too long and forgot to eat. Praise to the tosti and a wiki, because this toasted goodness deserves a blog.

Ingredients

The original dutch recepe is without the pineapple but we like to make a variation. You can also leave out the pineapple if you prefer.

  • 4 white bread
  • 2 slices of ham
  • 2 slices of cheese
  • 2 slices of canned pineapple
  • Ketchup

Steps

  1. First, arrange the cheese, ham and pineapple between the slices of bread.
  2. Next, Melt a tablespoon of butter in a non-stick skillet and place the sandwich in the middle of the pan on low heat. When the bottom side of the sandwich is golden brown, carefully flip it over.
  3. Then, Continue to fry until the other side is now brown to. Remove from pan.
  4. Cut in half, dip in ketchup and eat. Enjoy this dutchman's favorite lunch.
